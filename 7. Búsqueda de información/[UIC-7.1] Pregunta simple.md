# [UIC-7.1] Pregunta simple

## Input

How often should teeth cleaning take place?

## Output (Perplexity)

Most adults should have a professional teeth cleaning about every 6 months, but the ideal schedule depends on your individual risk for gum disease and tooth decay. [dentsanaclinic](https://dentsanaclinic.com/en/professional-dental-cleaning-every-each-quantity/)

### General guideline

- For people with healthy gums and low risk: a cleaning every 6 months is the usual recommendation. [healthline](https://www.healthline.com/health/dental-and-oral-health/how-often-should-you-get-your-teeth-cleaned)
- At minimum, you should see a dentist at least once a year for an exam, usually combined with a cleaning. [healthline](https://www.healthline.com/health/dental-and-oral-health/how-often-should-you-get-your-teeth-cleaned)

### When you may need more frequent cleanings

- History of gum disease (gingivitis or periodontitis): often every 3–4 months as part of maintenance. [levesquedentistry](https://www.levesquedentistry.com/2025/06/13/how-often-do-you-really-need-a-dental-cleaning/)
- High tartar buildup, smokers, diabetes, pregnancy, or many cavities: dentist may advise every 3–4 months. [blvddentistry](https://www.blvddentistry.com/how-often-should-you-have-dental-cleaning/)
- After deep cleaning for periodontitis: maintenance cleanings every 3–4 months are common. [whmcny](https://whmcny.org/blog/dental/deep-teeth-cleaning-frequency/)

### Individual variation

- National and professional bodies emphasize that the exact interval should be personalized after your dentist evaluates your mouth and risk factors. [bagnallfamilydentistry](https://bagnallfamilydentistry.com/blog/dental-cleaning-from-general-dentist-cip132/)
- In some health systems, the recommended interval between dental check‑ups (with cleaning as needed) can range from 3 months up to 2 years, depending on how healthy your teeth and gums are. [nhs](https://www.nhs.uk/live-well/healthy-teeth-and-gums/dental-check-ups/)

As a practical rule: aim for twice‑yearly cleanings unless your own dentist has advised a shorter or longer interval based on your situation.
