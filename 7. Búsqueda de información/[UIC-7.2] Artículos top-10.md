### Key Conclusions
Survival rates for occluso-proximal restorations in primary teeth range 25-50% at 2 years with GICs/ART, improving with experienced operators and brands like Ketac Molar. Composites and bulk-fills perform similarly or better in permanent teeth, but adhesives are crucial for proximal margins to reduce failure. Occlusal factors (e.g., contacts, schemes) and lesion site influence thresholds but not pulpotomy success (93%+). Simplified techniques (no polyacrylic acid preconditioning) show promise without compromising efficacy. [onlinelibrary.wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/iej.13588)
